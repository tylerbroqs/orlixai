01 The problem with AI today

Everyone has access to the same models. If your edge is "knowing how to prompt," that edge is shared by every other person using the same model. The loop looks like this:

re-explain → generate → copy → paste → verify → repeat ∞
−Context lives in their UI — switch vendors, lose everything
+Memory is portable, versioned, and stays with you
−Rules reset every session, you re-explain constantly
+Policies persist — set a rule once, enforced everywhere
−Output is a draft — you still send, check, follow up
+Actions execute and are verified against the goal
−No record of why a suggestion was made
+Every decision ships with a reviewable receipt
−Your accumulated context can't be exported
+Swap models anytime — your system stays intact

02 How Orlix works

Orlix runs a five-step governance loop continuously. Each step produces a structured output that feeds the next — building a compounding system over time.

01
Observe
Watches your calendar, inbox, goals, and connected tools in real time. Feeds a living world model — not a snapshot, a stream.
signal: calendar blocked 09:00–11:00 · goal: "Ship MVP" · blockers: 3 open
02
Decide
Weighs signals against your policies. Checks authority before any action — no surprises, no silent escalation.
policy: alert_if_blocker_gt_48h → triggered · authority: supervised
03
Act
Executes the task directly — or requests your approval first, depending on the authority tier you've configured.
action: "Priority alert sent to user" → ✓ delivered · receipt: #0847
04
Verify
Checks that the result actually achieved the goal — not just whether something was "sent" or "completed".
goal: "Unblock MVP" · outcome: user re-prioritised → verified ✓
05
Learn
Updates policy from real outcomes. Every approval, edit, and override becomes a signal. Policy changes are versioned — always reviewable.
policy v14 → v15: alert threshold 3d → 2d · reason: "user confirmed faster cadence"

03 Autonomy levels

A universal scale for AI autonomy — from read-only to a fully co-adaptive partner. Orlix is built to run at L5.

L1
InformationalRead-only. Answers questions, takes no action.
L2
ReactiveResponds within a single session, no memory beyond it.
L3
Mixed-initiativeCan suggest or warn, but a human drives every step.
L4
AgenticExecutes multi-step tasks autonomously — ends when the task does.
L5
Symbiotic — OrlixPersistent memory, a living model of your goals, policies that evolve from outcomes, full audit trail. The system doesn't end — it grows with you.

04 Memory & ownership

Chat history resets whenever a vendor decides. Memory in Orlix is yours by design — persistent, versioned, and exportable at any time.

portable
Nothing locked to a vendor. Export your memory, switch models or providers, keep everything you've built.
versioned
Every fact, goal, and policy carries a timestamp and version. Roll back if something changes for the worse.
sourced
Every belief Orlix holds traces back to an email, event, or decision. No hallucinated facts.
encrypted
Memory store is AES-256 encrypted at rest. Only you hold the keys — not us, not the model provider.
local-first
All processing can run on your machine. Cloud sync is opt-in, never required.

Orlix is model-agnostic. It works with any LLM that supports an API — Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, Llama, and local models via Ollama. Your memory, policies, and audit trail stay intact when you switch. The model is just a runtime; Orlix is the operating system.
By default, your memory and policies are stored locally on your machine, encrypted at rest with AES-256. Cloud sync is opt-in and zero-knowledge — we never see your memory contents. On the Autonomous plan you also get a dedicated cloud memory namespace, still encrypted.
Agents do tasks and stop. Orlix governs outcomes continuously. An agent runs a workflow; Orlix maintains a living model of your goals, enforces policy on every action, verifies that outcomes actually matched intent, and updates policy from what it learns. The loop never ends — it compounds.

11 Security & privacy

Your AI operating system should be the most trusted layer in your stack — not the least. We built Orlix with that constraint in mind from day one.

🔐
AES-256 encryption at rest
All memory, policies, and receipts are encrypted before being written to disk. Key management is yours — we never hold decryption keys.
🌐
Zero-knowledge cloud sync
Cloud backup is opt-in and end-to-end encrypted. Data is encrypted client-side before upload — our servers see ciphertext only.
🏠
Local-first architecture
The full governance loop runs on your machine. No data leaves your device unless you explicitly enable a cloud feature.
📋
No training on your data
Your memory, policies, and interaction history are never used to train models — ours or anyone else's.
🔍
Open source & auditable
The core engine is MIT licensed. Independent security researchers can audit the codebase — and we welcome responsible disclosure.
✅
SOC 2 Type II in progress
We're completing our SOC 2 Type II audit. GDPR and CCPA compliant. DPA available for business plans upon request.
